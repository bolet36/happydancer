EMTlive Privacy Statement

The Personal Health Records stored on a user's iPhone are protected by the TLS 1.3 protocol, a Security Key embeded in the EMTlive APP prior to the user entering personal health information. TLS 1.3 scrambles the data and makes it illegible without a secure biometric method of identification, such as the user's finger print or facial ID recognition as required by HIPAA for confidential information stored in Health Records. Opportunistic TLS (STARTTLS) is a protocol that helps provide privacy between communicating applications and their users during email delivery. When a server and client communicate, TLS ensures that no third party can overhear or tamper with any of the information accessed by the Emergency Medical Team (EMT) providing care for the injured person. Additionally, an iPhone is designed with encryption features that prevent anyone from accessing the personal health information stored on the iPhone without the user's selected passcode, fingerprint or facial ID.

EMTlive specifically does not:

1)  collect, store or have access to the user's personal health information;

 2) track the EMTs access of the user's information during it's delivery of care activities;

3) share user's health information with third parties for use in analytical tools, advertising networks or third party SDKs;

4) use remarketing or retargeting tracking code for use by third parties;

5) require users to grant access to their personal, private health information and data to anyone.

EMTlive specifically does:

Provide access to its Privacy Policy to ensure potential users understand how their health information will be protected before they decide to buy the APP.
